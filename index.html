The D.B. Cooper Mystery

The Hijacking - November 24, 1971

A man using the alias D.B. Cooper hijacked Northwest Orient Airlines Flight 305, demanding $200,000 and parachutes.

The Mysterious Jump

After receiving the ransom and parachutes, Cooper jumped from the Boeing 727 somewhere between Seattle and Reno.

What happened next remains one of aviation's greatest mysteries...

The Survival Theory

Some experts and investigators believe D.B. Cooper successfully survived the jump and escaped with the money.

"The possibility that Cooper was able to escape and live undetected has fueled ongoing speculation and investigation." - Encyclopaedia Britannica

Supporting evidence includes:

  • His apparent familiarity with aviation
  • The possibility he was a trained paratrooper
  • The strategic planning of the hijacking

The Survival Theory Continued

"Eric Ulis... believes the hijacker got away safely and is potentially still alive." - Oregon Public Broadcasting

The discovery of $5,800 of the ransom money along the Columbia River in 1980 has led to various theories about Cooper's fate, with some suggesting he successfully landed but lost some of the money during his escape.

The Death Theory

The FBI and many investigators believe Cooper did not survive the dangerous jump.

"Many in the FBI concluded that Cooper did not survive his high-risk jump." - Encyclopaedia Britannica

Factors supporting this theory:

  • Harsh weather conditions
  • Dangerous terrain
  • Darkness during the jump
  • The weight of the money

The Death Theory Continued

After 45 years of investigation, the FBI closed the case in 2016.

"The agency redirected resources... stating that investigative resources and manpower were diverted from programs that more urgently need attention." - New York Times
