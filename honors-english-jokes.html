Mr. Schiebe's Oath of Literary Absurdity

"This is not just a class. This is an odyssey through the labyrinth of metaphors, where every comma hides a secret and every typo has deep emotional significance."

By crossing the threshold of Honors English, you hereby swear to uphold the sacred and completely unnecessary traditions of overanalyzing everything you read, see, and occasionally dream about.

Your unbreakable vows include but are not limited to:

  • Assuming every story about a dog is secretly about the futility of life, and every story about life is secretly about a dog.
  • Reacting to even the mildest literary revelations as if you've just discovered the secret to immortality: *"Wait... the curtains were blue because he was SAD?!"*
  • Writing essays with titles like *"How a Single Raisin in Chapter Three Represents the Crushing Weight of Capitalism"*, because why not?
  • Laughing at jokes like, *"Why did the comma break up with the period? It felt too possessive!"* with the gusto of someone who actually finds that funny.
  • Accepting that the phrase "symbolic death" will now haunt your nightmares forever. (Spoiler: Itâ€™s always a symbolic death.)
  • Discussing whether the protagonist is relatable as if their entire fictional existence depends on your validation.

In conclusion, remember this: if you fail to analyze every minor detail in life as deeply as you do in this class, Mr. Schiebe may *politely* suggest that you spend eternity explaining the "deeper meaning" of why grocery store aisles are organized the way they are. (Hint: it's society.)
